Texas Hold Em User Play Business Rules

The following are the business rules for Texas Hold Em game play during the Alpha phase before authentication, table management and inventory management business rules are implemented.

User Identification and Play Options

1.       A user will provide an identifying name before entering or accept the default Guest

2.       Temporary (before authentication) - if the user name is already used in an active session (last activity time is 5 minutes), the user will need to enter a new name.

3.       A user may choose to do a practice game, enter an existing table id or start a new table

4.       A user who opts to play a practice games will play against three other dummy players. The actions by the dummy players will be random, i.e., a player with a bad hand is likely to bet as it is to fold.

5.       Temporary (before inventory management) - every user will be given 10,000 in chips upon first joining a table. A user may reset his inventory by resetting the entire table, in which case other players will have their balances reset.

Joining a Table

6.       If the table id does not exist, a new table will be created and the user given the first seat.

7.       If the table does exist and a game is not in progress, the user will be seated immediately but play cannot start until another user joins.

8.       If the table does exist and a game is in progress, the user will need to wait until the game is over. He can observe the game until it ends.

9.       If seats are available on a table the user will have the option to take the first available clockwise from the dealer.

10.   If more users join a table and there is contention for available seats, users will be placed on a wait queue and given the option of taking a seat in the order he or she joined the queue. The user has 5 seconds to take the seat before it is offered to the next player in the queue. If the user is not seated by the time it is his or her turn, the game will continue without that seat.

Leaving a table

11.   Temporary (before inventory management) - A user may leave the table at any time, any amount in the pot will be forfeit.

12.   If the second to last user leaves the table, the remaining player wins automatically but all game is stopped until another user joins.

13.   If a user rejoins the same table (and the table had continuous play since he left), his balance will as when he left the table.

Number of players

14.   The user can play against one to four other players.  The UI will always show four seats to allow a new player to come in and join. (Number to be increased to 7).

Playing Texas Hold Em Rules

The following are the standard Texas Hold Em rules. No-limit, pot-limit and tournament rules are not in scope for Alpha. Buying chips and capped bets are also not in scope for alpha.

Table Minimum

1.       Temporary (before inventory and table management) - The table minimum will be fixed at $200. In the future, players with different balances may join higher stake tables.

2.       The minimum bet for the third and fourth round of betting is double the table minimum.

Rotating Dealer

3.       Temporary (before table management) - The system will support rotating the dealer clockwise among the players. In the future, a user may join a table who has a system dealer.

4.       Temporary - The dealer will need to initiate a deal action by clicking  button. The system will make that option unavailable if no cards need to be dealt and will identify who and how many cards need to be given.

Blind Bets

5.       The two players at the left of the dealer must place blind bets, which are the minimum amounts they place on the table for use when it is their turn.

6.       The first player must place the small blind, which is the table minimum, and the second player posts the big blind, which is twice as much as the small blind.

7.       If only two players remain, the dealer places the small bet and the other player places the big bet. The dealer acts first before the flop and other player acts first after the flop.

8.       Usability: when a new game starts, the blind bets are immediately placed on behalf of the players. There is no delay.

Game Play (Betting Rounds and Dealing)

9.       Two cards are dealt by the dealer to each player after the blind bets are placed, starting with the player to the left.

10.   Usability: Moving the blind bets to the pot is an animation that starts, when the user makes the bet. The blind bets remain in the table until the players making those bets make their move.

11.   Usability: the act of dealing pre-flop cards is an animation for pacing purposes.

12.   The two cards by the user are placed faced up. The two cards by the other players are placed faced down.

13.   The player to the left of the player who placed the blind bet is the first to make a move.

14.   On the first round, a player has the option to call (bet the same amount as the last player), raise (up to twice the amount of the big blind for first player or ) or fold or raise as follows:

a.       First player may raise by betting double the amount of the big blind

b.      Other players may raise by betting the big blind amount plus the size of the previous bet.

15.   The players who made a blind bet use the blind bet when he or she makes a move.

16.   The player who placed the big blind has the option of checking on the first round.

17.   After every player makes a move, the first round is complete, and the dealer places the three community cards face up in the table (known as the fold).

18.   The second round starts with the first player to the left of the dealer.

19.   Player options on the second and third rounds are to Check, Raise, Call or Fold.

20.   The dealer places the fourth (turn) and fifth (river) community card on the table at the end of the second and third rounds respectively.

21.   The fourth and final round takes place after the river.

Timing

22.   Each player will be given 15 seconds to make a move.

23.   Usability: the amount of time remaining for a player to make a move will be visually displayed next to the player so that others (and the player him or herself) will see whose turn it is and  how much time they have to make a move or wait.

24.   A user who runs out of time for the first three times will be skipped. If this player runs out of time again, he or she will be kicked out of the game.

Showdown

1.       The showdown is triggered if all players but one fold before the end of the fourth round.

2.       The showdown starts at the end of the fourth betting round.

3.       The first player who made a bet in the final round has their cards shown first. If no one bet on the final round, the cards of the player closest to the left of the dealer are revealed first.

4.       Temporary - everyone else's cards are placed up, the hand for each player is identified, and the winner is identified. In the future, only the first player must show the card, other players will have the option conceding without revealing their cards.

5.       After all the cards are revealed, the winner's balance is updated with the size of the pot.

6.       Usability:  the showing of the cards is an animation, where the first and then the second card is revealed for each player, starting with the player to the left of the active player. Revealing each card will take one second.
